## Target Audience

*Briarwood shares the same customer personas as Nova Recovery Center — the same people need detox before they enter rehab. However, Briarwood catches them at an earlier, more acute stage.*

### Primary Personas — Males (Texas)

**M1 | Ethan (18-25) — Family-Driven Young Adult**
- Parent calls after crisis; on parent's insurance
- Decision driver: family boundaries + safety
- For detox: often in acute withdrawal or post-crisis, parent is making the call NOW
- Objections: "I can stop on my own" / cost concerns from parent

**M2 | Chris (26-32) — Employed, Married, Financially Stressed**
- Self or partner calls; secret use escalated
- Decision driver: keep job + restore trust
- For detox: needs fast, discreet medical detox that won't blow up his life
- Objections: "I can't take time off" / copay concerns

**M3 | Michael (33-55) — Provider on the Brink (Higher Medical Need)**
- Spouse calls after health scare; long-term use
- Decision driver: health + family stability + privacy
- For detox: HIGHEST medical need — may need Executive Detox for privacy
- Objections: "No one at work can know" / fear of withdrawal

### Primary Personas — Females (Texas)

**F1 | Alyssa (18-25) — Boundary-Pusher**
- Self or parent calls; escalating conflict
- For detox: may resist, needs to understand detox is safe and temporary

**F2 | Brianna (26-32) — The Balancer**
- Self or partner calls; holding it together barely
- For detox: wants discreet, clear timeline, cost clarity
- Objections: "How long will this take?" / fear of judgment

**F3 | Stephanie (33-55) — Primary Caregiver**
- Self, spouse, or family calls; kept it hidden for years
- For detox: worried about kids, needs logistical support during detox stay
- Objections: "I can't leave my kids" / fear of withdrawal / stigma

### Geography + Insurance
- Primary market: Texas (both genders)
- Out-of-state: BCBS and PPO policies only
- In-network with many insurance providers

## Core Content Framework: Market Awareness Levels + Funnel Stages

*Same framework as Nova — see the full doc at: https://docs.google.com/document/d/1STnE4R0egbLpLB_tjUcppbDdJHSXUUcrQnee7JxhPv4/edit*

### Adapted for Detox (differs from rehab)

| Label | What it means for detox specifically | Content strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Ready (3%)** | In crisis NOW — withdrawal symptoms, just had a scare, ready to come in today | Fast logistics — "beds available today," "here's what to bring," same-day admissions |
| **Comparing (17%)** | Researching detox options, asking about comfort, medical supervision, cost | Differentiation — Executive Detox, 24/7 medical care, home-like facility, insurance accepted |
| **Aware (20%)** | Knows they need to stop but terrified of withdrawal | Fear-of-withdrawal content — "detox doesn't have to be painful," medical supervision, comfort |
| **Early (60%)** | Family member watching someone deteriorate, person not yet admitting the problem | Educational — what withdrawal looks like, when detox becomes medically necessary, signs it's time |

### Content Mix for Detox Brand
- **BoF / Ready + Comparing (30% of content):** Higher than Nova — detox is a more acute/urgent decision
- **MoF / Aware (35% of content):** Withdrawal fear is the #1 barrier — address it heavily
- **ToF / Early (35% of content):** Education about when detox is needed, signs of dependence

## Content Pillars

1. **Withdrawal Education** — What detox is, what to expect, medical supervision, comfort measures, MAT
2. **Crisis-Moment Content** — Speaking to the person (or family member) in the acute moment of decision
3. **Fear-of-Withdrawal** — Directly addressing the #1 barrier: "Detox doesn't have to be painful"
4. **Service Awareness / CTA** — Beds available today, verify insurance, same-day admissions
5. **Family / Loved Ones** — Content for the person making the call on behalf of someone they love
6. **Trust & Proof** — Facility tours, staff introductions, national accreditation, comfort/amenities
7. **Post-Detox Pathway** — What comes after detox (transition to rehab/IOP — connects to Nova)
8. **Substance-Specific Education** — Alcohol withdrawal, opioid withdrawal, benzo withdrawal (match to service pages)

### Pillar Priority (Aligned to Call Goal)
- **High priority (60% of content):** Crisis-Moment, Withdrawal Fear, Service CTA, Family/Loved Ones
- **Medium priority (25% of content):** Trust & Proof, Substance-Specific Education
- **Supporting (15% of content):** Post-Detox Pathway, General Withdrawal Education

## Do / Don't

### DO
- Always include the correct platform-specific tracking phone number on every graphic
- Always include Briarwood logo (bird + text) on every graphic
- Emphasize COMFORT and SAFETY — these counter the #1 objection (fear of withdrawal)
- Speak to the urgency — detox is a NOW decision, not a "someday" decision
- Address the family member making the call (parents, spouses)
- Mention "beds available today" and "same-day admissions" frequently
- Highlight Executive Detox for privacy-concerned personas (M3/F3)
- Connect the dots: detox is Step 1, rehab (Nova) is Step 2
- Use location geo-tags (Austin, Houston, San Antonio)
- Post across all 4 platforms: Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn

### DON'T
- Don't use scary withdrawal imagery or fear-based messaging about what happens WITHOUT detox
- Don't post content without a CTA
- Don't show client faces (HIPAA)
- Don't use clinical jargon
- Don't make promises about pain-free detox (say "comfortable" and "medically supervised" instead)
- Don't confuse detox with rehab in messaging — Briarwood is detox only
- Don't ignore the medical necessity angle — for M3/F3, detox can be life-threatening without supervision
- Don't post generic motivational quotes without tying back to detox services
