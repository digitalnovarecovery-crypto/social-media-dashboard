## Target Audience

*Eudaimonia catches people AFTER detox and rehab — they've already been through Briarwood and/or Nova. But it also catches people who self-refer or come from other treatment centers.*

### Primary Personas — Adapted for Sober Living

**M1 | Ethan (18-25) — Post-Treatment Young Adult**
- Just completed rehab; parents want structured accountability before independence
- On parent's insurance (IOP may be covered); housing is private-pay
- Decision driver: parents want structure; Ethan wants freedom — Eudaimonia bridges both
- Objections: "I don't need a babysitter" / "I can live on my own"

**M2 | Chris (26-32) — Rebuilding After Treatment**
- Completed IOP or inpatient; needs stable housing while rebuilding career/relationship
- Decision driver: needs affordable, sober-supportive housing while working
- Key angle: employment assistance, work while living in sober home
- Objections: cost concerns / "I can just get my own apartment"

**M3 | Michael (33-55) — Provider Rebuilding Stability**
- Completed long-term treatment; needs to rebuild family trust and career
- Decision driver: demonstrate accountability to family/employer
- Key angle: drug testing with family-accessible results, three-phase program
- Objections: "I need privacy" / "I've been away long enough"

**F1/F2/F3 — Women's Sober Living**
- Similar patterns; additional considerations: childcare logistics, safety, gender-specific environment
- Key angle: women's-only homes, safe environment, clinical support

### Geography + Payment
- Primary market: Texas (both genders)
- Colorado Springs and Philadelphia: expanding markets
- **Sober living = private pay ($550-$1,800/month)** — NOT insurance-covered
- IOP services may be billable to insurance
- Important: content must address affordability upfront — this is a cost-conscious audience

## Core Content Framework: Market Awareness Levels (Adapted for Sober Living)

| Label | What it means for sober living | Content strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Ready (3%)** | Completing rehab, needs housing NOW, comparing options | Fast logistics — "beds available," pricing, virtual tour, move-in process |
| **Comparing (17%)** | Shopping sober living options — cost, location, rules, amenities | Differentiation — three-phase program, employment help, accountability, pricing |
| **Aware (20%)** | Knows sober living would help but has objections (cost, independence, stigma) | Objection handling — "sober living vs. going home," cost breakdown, success stories |
| **Early (60%)** | In treatment and not yet thinking about aftercare, OR family worried about what's next | Education — what sober living is, why it matters, relapse stats without structure |

### Content Mix for Sober Living Brand
- **BoF / Ready + Comparing (25% of content):** Tours, pricing transparency, move-in info
- **MoF / Aware (35% of content):** Objection handling, success stories, family-targeted
- **ToF / Early (40% of content):** Why sober living matters, transition education, community benefits

## Visual Photography Style

- **Lighting:** Bright, natural daylight — open and airy
- **Composition:** Interior home shots (living rooms, bedrooms, kitchens), exterior porches/gardens, neighborhood scenes
- **Background treatment:** Clean, minimal — let the homes speak for themselves
- **Subject framing:** Spaces, not faces — show the comfortable living environment
- **Colour temperature:** Warm but bright — greens and yellows reflecting growth
- **Mood:** Hopeful, fresh start, comfortable, community-oriented, HOME

## Do / Don't

### DO
- Always include the correct platform-specific tracking phone number
- Always include Eudaimonia logo
- Show REAL homes — potential residents want to see where they'll actually live
- Be transparent about pricing ($550-$1,800/month range)
- Emphasize accountability features (drug testing, three-phase program, 24/7 staff)
- Address the "what comes after rehab" question — this is Eudaimonia's entire value prop
- Speak to families: "You can see their test results online"
- Mention employment/education assistance — people need to rebuild their lives
- Highlight that there's no maximum stay — take the time you need
- Reference the continuum: Briarwood (detox) → Nova (rehab) → Eudaimonia (sober living)
- Use location geo-tags for each city

### DON'T
- Don't make sober living sound like a hospital or institution — it's a HOME
- Don't post without a CTA
- Don't show resident faces (privacy)
- Don't hide pricing — affordability is a selling point
- Don't use language that makes it sound like punishment or restriction
- Don't confuse sober living with detox or rehab in messaging
- Don't ignore the cost objection — address it directly
- Don't make it seem like residents are "locked in" — they work, go to school, live their lives
