## Core Content Framework: Market Awareness Levels + Funnel Stages

This framework governs HOW content is created and WHO it targets. Every piece of content must be mapped to an awareness level.

### The 3-17-20-60 Rule (where prospects' minds are at)
- **3%** — Ready to buy right now
- **17%** — Info gathering mode (comparing, reading reviews, researching)
- **20%** — Problem aware but don't know the solution yet
- **60%** — Not even problem aware

### Top/Middle/Bottom of Funnel (where they are in YOUR acquisition system)
- **Top (ToF)** — Entertaining, broad education content. Pulls people in. High reach.
- **Middle (MoF)** — Nurturing content: case studies, specific how-tos, results
- **Bottom (BoF)** — Conversion-focused: program breakdowns, CTAs ("book a call")

### Key Takeaways
1. These two frameworks are different things — most people conflate them
2. **Pair ToF with the 60%, MoF with 17-20%, BoF with the 3%**
3. On average it takes 7 hours of consumed content before someone buys — your job is to compress that cycle
4. The marketer willing to spend the most to acquire a customer wins — they dominate by consuming the 3-17% and working down to the 60%
5. Most treatment centers are only hitting BoF (3%), but there's a massive untapped market in the 60% who don't even realize they need treatment yet

### Adapted for Admissions

The awareness levels shift in a treatment context — people calling in are already somewhat aware of the problem:

| Label | What it really means in admissions | Content / follow-up strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Ready (3%)** | Insurance verified, wants to come in, just needs logistics | Fast, action-focused — "here's what to bring, here's day 1" |
| **Comparing (17%)** | Shopping multiple facilities, asking about amenities, cost, reviews | Differentiation — testimonials, what sets you apart, tour offer |
| **Aware (20%)** | Knows they need help, has a clear objection blocking them (cost, fear, family) | Objection-specific nurture — address the barrier directly |
| **Early (60%)** | Family caller, person in denial, very early stage, not ready | Educational, no pressure — normalize treatment, small steps. No CTA pressure — goal is to stay in their orbit until they move themselves up the funnel |

### How This Maps to Content Creation

**Pipeline × Touch # × Awareness Label → message variant**

Each piece of content should be tagged with its awareness level so the right message reaches the right person:
- **Ready** → Current fast-track automation (unchanged)
- **Comparing** → Differentiation sequence (testimonials, tours, what sets Nova apart)
- **Aware** → Objection-specific nurture (address the specific barrier directly)
- **Early** → Removed from hard-sell. Long-play educational content. No CTA pressure. Goal: stay in orbit until they re-engage meaningfully

### Content Mix by Awareness Level (for 60 calls/month goal)
- **BoF / Ready + Comparing (20% of content):** Direct CTAs, insurance verification, "what to expect" logistics, testimonials, facility tours, cost clarity
- **MoF / Aware (40% of content):** Objection-handling, persona-specific pain points, family-targeted content, "I can't take 30 days off" answers
- **ToF / Early (40% of content):** Educational, normalize recovery, broad reach, no hard sell — the 60% untapped market that nobody else is targeting

### Critical Insight
> "The long-play drip is essentially a separate product to build — but it's also where the biggest untapped revenue is. The 3% is fought over by every treatment center. The 60% is almost nobody's strategy."

Social content should always overwrite with the most recent awareness determination. People regress — someone who was "Ready" last Tuesday might have had a family blowup, lost their insurance, or just gotten scared. The last signal is the most current signal of where their head is at.

## Content Pillars

1. **Recovery Education** — What addiction is, how treatment works, what to expect (detox, inpatient, IOP, sober living)
2. **Persona Pain Points** — Content that speaks directly to each persona's situation and objections (targeted at the person or their family member making the call)
3. **Service Awareness / CTA** — Direct calls to action: call now, verify insurance, same-day admissions available
4. **Motivational / Inspirational** — Quotes, milestones, sobriety celebrations (keep but tie back to CTA)
5. **Objection Handling** — Address common fears: "I can't take 30 days off," cost/insurance, privacy, "I tried before," leaving kids
6. **Trust & Proof** — Testimonials, staff introductions (many in recovery themselves), facility tours, amenities
7. **Family / Loved Ones** — Content for parents, spouses, partners who are researching on behalf of someone they love
8. **Community & Events** — Local involvement, awareness months, seasonal tie-ins

### Pillar Priority (Aligned to 60 Calls/Month Goal)
- **High priority (60% of content):** Persona Pain Points, Service CTA, Objection Handling, Family/Loved Ones
- **Medium priority (25% of content):** Trust & Proof, Recovery Education
- **Supporting (15% of content):** Motivational/Inspirational, Community & Events

## Do / Don't

### DO
- Always include the correct platform-specific tracking phone number on every graphic (see Phone Numbers section — each platform has its own number)
- Always include Nova logo on every graphic
- Use gold (#efc732) as the signature social color
- Speak directly to the persona's situation — use their language
- Address the caller (often a family member, not just the person struggling)
- End captions with a clear CTA (call, verify insurance, link in bio)
- Use nature/outdoor imagery — roads, forests, sunlight
- Keep the tone warm, direct, and non-judgmental
- Mention specific services (detox, inpatient rehab, IOP, sober living) in every post
- Use location geo-tags on posts (Austin, Houston, San Antonio, etc.)
- Post across all 4 platforms: Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn

### DON'T
- Don't post content without a CTA — every post should drive toward a call or insurance verification
- Don't use stock photos of obviously staged "therapy" sessions
- Don't show client faces (privacy/HIPAA)
- Don't use clinical/medical jargon that alienates families doing late-night research
- Don't make promises about outcomes or cure rates
- Don't post generic motivational quotes without tying them back to services
- Don't ignore the family member — they're often the one scrolling at 2am
- Don't use fear-based or shame-based messaging
- Don't reference specific insurance companies in public posts
- Don't post content that could be interpreted as medical advice

## Tone of Voice

- **Warm but direct** — We care deeply, and we don't sugarcoat. Recovery is hard, and we honor that.
- **Non-judgmental** — No shame, no scare tactics. Meet people where they are.
- **Action-oriented** — We encourage the next step, not perfection. "Make the call. Show up. Tell the truth."
- **Inclusive of family** — We speak to the person struggling AND to the mom, spouse, or partner researching at 2am.
- **Confident but humble** — We know recovery works because many of us have lived it. But we never promise miracles.
- **Conversational** — Not clinical. Write like you're talking to a friend who's scared and needs reassurance.
